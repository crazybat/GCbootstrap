As emails are not stored in a central place, the information can be lost when an employee leaves the department. |2=Why do we need GCdocs?}} {{Collapse |1=GCdocs offers us a number of benefits: *One shared repository: Allowing you to access the DFO information you need from anywhere across the Department; *Version control: Will make it easier to know that you’re working on the most recent version; *Powerful search capability: Giving you much faster searches that will save time usually spent hunting for information; *Collaboration environment: As GCdocs is accessible by your colleagues across the Department, you will no longer have to send attachments out for comments by email and then have to merge all the comments; and; *Security: Allows you to store up to and including Protected B documents on GCdocs. |2=How will GCdocs benefit us?}} {{Collapse |1=GCdocs is the Government of Canada Electronic Document Records Management Solution (EDRMS) that will optimize how information is saved, shared, found, protected, organized and disposed of. Public Works and Government Services will provide IT support to the system for all Government Departments, freeing up Departmental resources to work on other things. This does not include what employees have stored in email or on external drives or internal hard drives.

On an individual level, employees are encouraged to clean up their email inboxes and personal storage spaces (such as U: drives) as much as possible. These measures also support information management best practices and other transformation initiatives such as the Email Transformation Initiative (ETI). |2=How can directorates and individuals prepare for GCdocs implementation?}} {{Collapse |1= '''A''' - It is not recommended that large amounts of finalized records be migrated into GCdocs without the assistance of IM and a tool to ensure that key metadata components are retained. The migration strategy will ensure that that the necessary metadata is mapped and integrity maintained when documents are migrated into GCdocs from EKME and shared drives. Directorates should also raise these issues when the Information Management business analysts meet with them to discuss their requirements for their GCdocs folder structure. |2=Will the document date and other metadata be maintained when documents are migrated into GCdocs?}}

===How Will GCdocs Help Me with Email Management?=== {{Collapse |1= '''A''' - No. Many DFO employees have gotten accustomed to using MS Outlook as an information management tool because of its great search capacity; however, this will soon become increasingly problematic for individuals. Personal Storage Table (PST) files will soon no longer be supported and thus individuals will no longer be able to open them or repair them should they become corrupted. In addition, inbox sizes will soon be capped at 1.5 GB. Many from di within Outlook making the process easy, depending on personal preference. Employees will quickly learn to view email as either transitory (can be deleted) or as an information resource of business value (should be saved in an appropriate, accessible folder within GCdocs) instead of just keeping every email and relying on the search function within MS Outlook. |2=Will e-mail be the hardest to migrate to GCdocs? }} {{Collapse |1= '''A''' - The search function in GCdocs is very powerful. It has full-text search capability which means that GCdocs will look for the key words you provided in a document name and in the body of documents and emails. The use of email folders will make searching easier as well because more fields are viewed. |2= How easy will it be to find an email I just saved in GCdocs?}} {{Collapse |1= '''A''' - For maintaining the integrity of the email (legal purposes), you should not change the subject header on an email when you save it into GCdocs. It is possible, however it is not recommended. It is important to properly name emails at the time of creation so that the subject header reflects the email content. You can also add details of the email into the description field so that the email is easy to find. Both the content of the email and the description field are full-text searchable. The use of email folders allows one to rename the record without changing the subject line. |2=Can you change the subject header on an email? }} {{Collapse |1= '''A''' - Saving an encrypted email into GCdocs should be avoided: In the event that the owner of that email leaves the Department, there is no way to decrypt the message, rendering the content useless. Emails should be decrypted prior to saving them in GCdocs. Remember, permissions can be restricted once in GCdocs to ensure proper security of the information contained therein. Please note that that key Information Management employees will be able to adjust these permissions accordingly if the owner were to leave the Department. Information up to and including Protected B can be saved in GCdocs without encryption, with the folder permissions/restrictions in place to ensure that the information is available to only those who need access. |2=Can we save encrypted emails to GCdocs? }}

===GCdocs File Management=== {{Collapse |1= '''A''' - No, not yet. The folder structure, or file plan for DFO / Canadian Coast Guard is currently in developments collaboratively with Directorates in 2018/19. Library and Archives Canada (LAC) has developed a generic folder structure for Internal Services (e.g. human resources, finance) however, this will have to be adjusted through consultation with directorates to meet DFO needs. In addition, a folder structure for our programs and operations will need to be created during this period. The '[https://gcdocs.gc.ca/dfo-mpo/llisapi.dll/open/681311 Open for Business]' principles will be used to create the folder structure. |2=Does the folder structure, or file plan for GCdocs already exist? }} {{Collapse |1= '''A''' - The DFO folder structure will be developed in consultation with directorates who will be prompted to request regional input and identify regional variances. Key regional contacts will also be asked to provide input prior to folder structure creation within GCdocs. As this structure is in development, no further information on the structure, or regional variances, is yet available to share. |2=How will the Regions be included in the GCdocs folder structure development? }} {{Collapse |1= '''A''' - GCdocs has the capability to see a document through each stage of the information management life cycle, including disposition. Please note, disposition will not occur without the final approval by the Office of Primary Interest (OPI). When records have met their retention period, a report will be generated and sent to the OPI (e.g. Director of the unit) requesting approval for their disposition. Existing retention and disposition schedules will be incorporated into the folder structure when setting up each folder. Each document within a folder will inherit its retention period and its permissions however, an OPI can decide to keep a document longer than its established retention period if it remains to be of business value. |2=Will disposition be automated in GCdocs? }} {{Collapse |1= '''A''' - The plan is to run reports on a quarterly basis. The frequency may need to change once the volume of information grows. |2=How often will this Disposition Reports be sent to the OPI for review and approval? }} {{Collapse |1= '''A''' - Disposition date will be determined by the relevant retention and disposition schedule and the triggers previously identified. These may be lengthened at the request of the appropriate directorate if necessary, but they cannot be shortened without a complete review of the applicable retention and disposition schedule due to the legal implications. |2=How will the disposition date be determined? }} {{Collapse |1= '''A''' - The management of versions is quite versatile and comprehensive in GCdocs. Whether a directorate will use linear (1, 2, 3, etc.) or major and minor (1.0, 1.1, 1.2 1.3, 2.0, etc.) versions will be decided by individual directorates. While the Information Management Branch will provide guidelines and best practices, it recognizes that version management is highly preferential and the Branch will not enforce one way or another. |2=Are we going to use Major and Minor Versions? }} {{Collapse |1='''A''' - No, it cannot. The document audit function (found under the properties of the document) will list every action taken and this list cannot be deleted. You can however manage versions of the document, which is actually a very good IM best practice. |2=Can the document history be wiped? }} {{Collapse |1= '''A''' - GCdocs will allow you to create subfolders once you are down to a certain level of the folder structure. However, it would be wise to limit the number of subfolders your work unit creates to promote a better filing structure and avoid having to drill down too many layers to get to your documents. The GCdocs team will work with directorates to create a folder structure that best meets business requirements. |2=Will we be able to create subfolders? }} {{Collapse |1= '''A''' - No. As with folder structure, having a set naming convention can help reduce the guessing game for employees who are searching for specific documents. A naming convention will not be prescribed, but rather suggested as a best practice. It will not be enforced by the IM Branch, but can be enforced within a directorate. Finding documents in GCdocs will be much more intuitive with the new folder structure and if required, the search function for findings misplaced documents is so refined that it can find key words in titles and document content. You can also search using the Boolean Search function (AND, OR, NOT) to refine your search. |2=Will there be a naming convention and/or Thesaurus as part of the GCdocs roll-out? }} {{Collapse |1= '''A''' - There will only be one repository for all of DFO. The central repository will be accessible by all DFO GCdocs users. If and when the Secret instance of GCdocs becomes available to DFO, select users may have one other. |2=How many repositories will there be for DFO? }} {{Collapse |1= '''A''' - Yes. Those information resources that are migrated into GCdocs from EKME and shared drives will be accessible through GCdocs and those remaining in shared drives will be ‘read-only’ for continued accessibility. Enduring Information Resources of Business Value (IRBVs) will be sent to Library and Archives Canada (LAC) electronically for continued storage. |2=Will GCdocs users be able to access legacy files? }}
